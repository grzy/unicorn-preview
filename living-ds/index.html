01The mark
The unicorn is built from terminal block glyphs, not an illustration. The same pixel form appears in the terminal banner, the app preloader, and the brief. The horn is lilac; the body is peach. It is rendered as text or solid pixels, never as a smooth vector, so it always reads as made of pixels.

03The signature move · gradient stroke, faded corners
Peach to lilac is the one gesture that signals Unicorn. As of June 2026 it runs along the bottom-left→top-right diagonal and fades to transparent at the top-right and bottom-left corners -- the single stroke carries both the hue shift and the corner fade. The exact same edge runs on the app icon, the window's glass edge, and every panel, so the whole product reads as one object. The ring uses a mask-composite technique so the gradient sits on the edge, not the fill. Light does the work; strokes are avoided everywhere else.

04Typography
Two families. Ubuntu Sans for body and reading; Ubuntu Sans Mono for display, labels, and anything that should feel like the terminal. Three sizes only: meta, body, display. Restraint over a type ramp.

06Glass & geometry
Panels are liquid glass: a translucent dark fill at rgba(22,22,22,0.78) with a 26px backdrop blur. No heavy strokes, no drop shadows. One 1px peach to lilac hairline under each header is the only decoration. Radii: 18px for panels, 6px for chips and inputs.

09The brief
Unicorn composes a creative brief, not a recap. Structured as Direction (where the quest is heading), Signal (the tools, themes, and links your work revealed), and Work (the kept moments, experiments, and commits). It opens with a one-line read of what you're becoming and closes on a gradient line: Make something this week. Self-contained HTML, shareable.

10Motion principles
  • No AI lift. Photos and cards don't scale or brighten on hover. Static is the goal; the lift is the tell.
  • Delicate arrival, decisive exit. Hover effects ease in slower than they ease out, so nothing lingers.
  • Ramp both ways. Nothing pops in or cuts out. Everything fades, with generous easing.
  • Light, not strokes. Depth comes from gradient, blur, and glass, not from outlines.
  • Respect reduced-motion. Every animation gates behind the OS preference.
11Desktop treatment
In the Electron app the window is transparent: panels float on your real desktop, beside the terminal. HUD mode keeps Unicorn on top and lets clicks pass through the gaps to the terminal underneath, so Claude Code and Unicorn are usable at once. The preloader, panels, and greeting all soften so the desktop stays visible.
